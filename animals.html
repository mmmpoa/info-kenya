Information: Kenyan Animals!

General Info Animals Culture

Kenya is home to a wide variety of wildlife, making it one of the top safari destinations in the world. Its national parks and reserves protect many iconic African species, and the country's diverse habitats—from savannahs to forests and wetlands—support an incredible range of animals. These animals attract tourists, researchers, and conservationists from around the globe.

Animals found in kenya:

  • African elephants: the largest land animals, found in parks like Amboseli
  • Lions: including tree: climbing lions seen in Lake Nakuru National Park
  • Leopards: elusive big cats often spotted in Maasai Mara and Sambur
  • Rhinos: both black and white rhinos, protected in places like Ol Pejeta
  • Buffaloes: strong and social animals, often seen in large herds
  • Giraffes: including the endangered Rothschild's giraffe
  • Zebras and wildebeests: famous for the Great Migration in the Maasai Mara
  • Cheetahs: the fastest land animals, often seen in open plains
  • Hippos and crocodiles: commonly found in rivers and lakes
  • Hundreds of bird species: including flamingos, eagles, and hornbills
